Section 1 — Purpose and commitment

The Organization is committed to a workplace—whether physical, remote, or hybrid—where everyone is treated with dignity and respect. Harassment, discrimination, and abusive conduct are not tolerated.

This policy applies to all team members, contractors engaged in a comparable capacity, and others interacting with the Organization’s workforce in work-related contexts.

Section 2 — Definitions (practical, non-exhaustive)

2.1 Harassment

Harassment means unwelcome conduct related to a protected characteristic or status where applicable law defines protected characteristics—or unwelcome conduct that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive environment based on the reasonable perception of the person affected.

Examples may include: slurs, stereotypes, unwanted comments about appearance, bullying masked as “jokes,” ostracism tied to identity, or persistent unwelcome messaging.

2.2 Sexual harassment

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when submission to such conduct is made a condition of employment or when such conduct unreasonably interferes with work or creates an offensive environment.

2.3 Abuse of power

Misuse of hierarchy, access, or scheduling authority to pressure, intimidate, or isolate someone may constitute harassment or misconduct even when it does not map neatly to a single category.

Section 3 — Respectful communication

Team members are expected to communicate professionally in chat, email, video calls, and code review. Disagreement should focus on ideas and work product, not personal attacks. “Heat of the moment” language is not an excuse for demeaning conduct.

Section 4 — Reporting

4.1 Channels

Reports may be made to:

(1) Your manager, unless the concern involves your manager—in that case use option (2) or (3).  
(2) People Operations, through the confidential intake form or listed alias.  
(3) An alternate reporting line, published annually for cases involving People Operations leadership.

Emergency safety issues should follow local emergency services guidance first.

4.2 Confidentiality and good faith

Reports should be made in good faith. The Organization will protect confidentiality to the extent consistent with a fair investigation and legal obligations. Anonymous reports may be accepted where the system allows; anonymous reports may limit the Organization’s ability to investigate.

Section 5 — Investigations (outline)

Upon receiving a report, People Operations or designated investigators may: acknowledge receipt where appropriate without guaranteeing a specific timeline; conduct interviews and review relevant records (messages, calendars, work artifacts) proportionate to the concern; recommend outcomes ranging from coaching to separation, consistent with findings and policy.

Team members are expected to cooperate respectfully with investigations, without falsifying information.

Section 6 — Non-retaliation

Retaliation against someone for reporting, participating in an investigation, or supporting a good-faith report is prohibited. Retaliation may include unwarranted performance penalties, social isolation, reassignment designed to punish, or threats.

Section 7 — Remedies and outcomes

Outcomes depend on severity, patterns, and accountability. The Organization may implement training, monitoring, role changes, or termination of relationships where warranted.

Section 8 — Interaction with other policies

Conduct that violates this policy may also violate the Information Security & Acceptable Use Policy (for example misuse of systems to harass). Multiple policies may apply.
