Section 1 — Purpose

This policy describes how the Organization approaches cash compensation, benefits at a high level, and performance-related rewards. Individual offer letters and local plans may add detail.

Section 2 — Pay cycles and base pay

Base pay is generally reviewed annually. Pay dates and currency are specified in local payroll documentation. Overtime or premium pay, if applicable, follows local law and role classification.

Section 3 — Variable cash and bonuses

3.1 Performance alignment

The Organization may operate discretionary bonus programs tied to company results, team outcomes, and individual performance. Bonus eligibility, target amounts (if any), and timing are communicated at the start of each plan year or quarter, depending on program design.

3.2 Individual contribution

Individual bonuses, when offered, reflect manager assessment of impact, alignment with values, and delivery against goals. The Organization expects managers to consider both visible outcomes (shipped work, revenue support, reliability) and collaboration (supporting peers, documentation, mentoring).

3.3 Calibration (high level)

Before bonus recommendations are finalized, leadership may conduct a cross-team review to reduce bias and check for consistency. Details of calibration meetings, rubrics, and documentation standards are maintained internally and may evolve without a full policy revision.

Note for team members: if you have questions about how your performance was assessed for compensation decisions, discuss them first with your manager, then with People Operations.

Section 4 — Equity (illustrative only)

Some roles may be eligible for equity-style incentives as described in separate grant documentation. This policy does not define vesting, tax treatment, or exercise mechanics. Those topics are covered only in individualized documents and local supplements.

Section 5 — Benefits overview

Benefits (health, retirement, and similar programs) vary by country and provider. Summaries are provided at onboarding and during open enrollment. The Organization aims to offer competitive packages within budget constraints described in periodic leadership communications.

Section 6 — Expenses and stipends

Role-specific stipends (for example home office or learning) may exist. Unless a stipend is explicitly approved in writing, expenses follow the expense policy attached to finance systems.

Section 7 — Pay equity and appeals

The Organization commits to monitoring pay equity at an aggregate level and addressing issues found through People Operations and leadership review. Individual pay appeals are handled through a confidential process initiated via People Operations.
