Section 1 — Purpose and scope

This handbook describes general employment practices for team members of the Organization. Policies apply worldwide, subject to stricter local mandatory law where applicable. Where local law is stricter, local law controls for team members working in that location.

The Organization operates as a global, distributed technology team (approximately 80–150 people). This handbook is a high-level guide; detailed rules for compensation, security, and remote work appear in separate policies referenced here.

Section 2 — Employment relationship

Team members are engaged according to individual agreements and local requirements. Nothing in this handbook creates a contract of employment where local law would not otherwise recognize one, and nothing guarantees continued employment for any fixed period.

Section 3 — Working hours and availability

3.1 Core collaboration hours

Unless a role-specific exception is approved in writing, team members are expected to be available for live collaboration during core hours defined as 13:00–17:00 UTC on weekdays. Individual teams may agree on narrower windows within this band.

3.2 Flexible scheduling

Outside core hours, team members may distribute working time flexibly in agreement with their manager, consistent with role needs and customer commitments.

Section 4 — Time away from work

4.1 Paid time off (PTO)

Team members accrue PTO according to their offer letter or local schedule, whichever is more favorable where legally required. PTO requests should be submitted at least ten (10) business days in advance when possible. Managers approve PTO subject to coverage and project deadlines.

4.2 Leave for health or family

Requests for extended leave (medical, parental, or other) should be routed through People Operations. Documentation requirements vary by location; People Operations will confirm what applies.

Section 5 — Remote and hybrid work (summary)

Remote and hybrid arrangements are an important part of how the Organization works. Detailed expectations—including scheduling, equipment, and expenses—are set out in the Remote & Hybrid Work Guidelines.

For planning and fairness, the Organization uses a simple baseline for scheduled remote work:

Scheduled remote work: team members may work remotely for up to eight (8) scheduled days per calendar month, unless a different arrangement is approved in writing by People Operations.

“Scheduled remote work” means days pre-identified in the team’s planning tool (or equivalent) as remote workdays. Ad hoc remote days for illness, emergencies, or one-off needs are handled under the Remote & Hybrid Work Guidelines and may follow different notification rules.

Important: if any other policy or guideline appears to describe remote work differently, team members should follow the most specific written instruction from People Operations for their location and role. Managers may not waive mandatory safety or security requirements.

Section 6 — Performance and conduct

6.1 Standards

Team members are expected to collaborate professionally, meet commitments, and follow applicable policies (including information security and respectful workplace policies).

6.2 Reviews

Performance is reviewed on a regular cycle described in the Compensation & Benefits Policy. Feedback should be documented in the performance system when available.

Section 7 — Reporting concerns

Concerns about workplace conduct, ethics, or policy violations may be reported through channels described in the Anti-Harassment & Respectful Workplace Policy. Retaliation for good-faith reporting is prohibited.

Section 8 — Changes to this handbook

The Organization may update this handbook. Material changes will be communicated through internal channels. Continued employment or engagement after notice constitutes acknowledgment of updates, except where local law requires separate consent.
