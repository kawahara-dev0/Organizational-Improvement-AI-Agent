Section 1 — Purpose

These guidelines operationalize flexible work for the Organization’s distributed teams. They complement the Employee Handbook and security policies.

Section 2 — Principles

Outcomes over location: work should be judged on results, collaboration, and reliability—not solely on visibility in an office.

Team coverage: teams need predictable overlap for decisions, reviews, and customer support.

Security: remote work must not weaken customer trust or data protection.

Section 3 — Remote and hybrid patterns

Team members may work in a hybrid pattern (some days co-located where hubs exist, some days remote) or fully remote where role and location allow.

Managers and teams should agree on: expected overlap hours (see handbook core hours); how on-call or urgent incidents are handled; how presence is signaled (status in chat, calendar blocks).

Section 4 — Scheduling flexibility and manager discretion

4.1 Team needs first

Managers may exercise reasonable discretion to adjust remote schedules when needed for customer incidents, launch windows, mentorship, onboarding, or fairness within the team. This includes temporarily asking team members to align on-site or align on synchronous remote days when projects require tight coordination.

Discretion should be applied consistently and communicated with as much notice as practical.

4.2 Relationship to the handbook baseline

The Employee Handbook includes a scheduled remote work baseline for planning and fairness. These guidelines describe how teams apply that baseline day to day, including ad hoc remote days for illness, childcare disruptions, or short-term personal needs.

When a team’s operating model requires more flexibility than a strict monthly count would allow—especially for globally distributed squads—managers should document the team pattern in the planning tool and confirm alignment with People Operations when unsure.

Practical note: if you believe your manager’s scheduling direction conflicts with written policy, first discuss it with your manager, then escalate to People Operations with specific dates and requests.

Section 5 — Equipment and workspace

Team members working remotely are expected to maintain a private, quiet space where confidential conversations and screens are not exposed to household members or public view.

Equipment stipends, if any, are described in offer materials and finance policies—not in this document.

Section 6 — Expenses

Ordinary home utilities are not reimbursed. Approved business expenses (for example required travel or approved peripherals) follow the expense process.

Section 7 — Performance and visibility

Remote work should not penalize team members in performance discussions solely because they are not in an office. Managers should document impact using artifacts (designs, commits, tickets, customer feedback). At the same time, team members are responsible for proactive communication when work is blocked or priorities shift.

Section 8 — Cross-border work

Working temporarily from a country other than your primary work location may have tax, immigration, and data implications. Advance written approval is required before working more than fourteen (14) cumulative days per year from a non-primary country, unless People Operations publishes a shorter threshold.
