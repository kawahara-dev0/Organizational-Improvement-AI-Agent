Section 1 — Purpose

This policy sets baseline rules for protecting the Organization’s systems, data, and customers. It applies to all team members and to contractors with access to Organization assets.

Section 2 — Accounts and authentication

Use only accounts issued to you. Do not share credentials. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required where enabled. Report suspected compromise immediately to the security alias.

Section 3 — Acceptable use of systems

Organization-provided or approved tools (email, chat, code hosts, ticketing) must be used for work purposes and reasonable personal use that does not create risk or violate law.

Prohibited examples (non-exhaustive): accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content; attempting to bypass security controls or scan networks without authorization; installing unapproved software that is not on the approved catalog, except where temporary exceptions are granted in writing; using Organization resources to harass others (see the Anti-Harassment & Respectful Workplace Policy).

Section 4 — Customer and confidential data

Access customer or sensitive internal data only on a need-to-know basis for your role. Do not store customer data on personal devices unless explicitly approved under a written exception. Use approved channels for transferring data; avoid personal email or consumer cloud sync for work secrets.

Section 5 — Devices

5.1 Organization-managed devices

If you receive a managed laptop or phone, follow enrollment steps, allow security updates, and return equipment on departure.

5.2 Bring-your-own-device (BYOD)

Where BYOD is permitted, enrollment in mobile device management (MDM) may be required. If you decline enrollment, access to certain systems may be withheld.

Section 6 — Remote and travel use

When working outside primary locations, protect screens and conversations in public spaces. Use VPN or equivalent where required. Lost or stolen devices must be reported within twenty-four (24) hours.

Section 7 — Incident reporting

Suspected incidents (phishing success, accidental data exposure, malware) should be reported to the security alias without fear of retaliation for good-faith reporting. Deliberate concealment of incidents may result in disciplinary action.

Section 8 — Monitoring (high level)

The Organization may monitor systems for security and compliance in line with applicable law and internal notices. Personal use does not imply privacy from all monitoring on Organization systems.

Section 9 — Policy violations

Violations may result in suspension of access, investigation, and employment or contract consequences. Some violations may trigger legal reporting obligations.
